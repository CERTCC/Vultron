## Summary

A three-actor demo scenario where a Finder reports directly to Vendor1, and Vendor1 then
engages Vendor2 without any coordinator. This exercises the direct vendor-to-vendor
coordination path in the CVD protocol, including supply-chain upstream/downstream
relationships.

## Motivation

The existing demos cover FV (two-actor) and FCV/FVCV (coordinator-mediated). No scenario
demonstrates a purely vendor-to-vendor chain, which is a realistic and important CVD
pattern (e.g., upstream/downstream supplier relationships in modern software supply chains).

This scenario is also the foundation for supply-chain disclosure: a vendor who learns of
a vulnerability discovers it affects downstream components or suppliers, and expands the
case to those parties without involving a coordinator.

For the related case where a vendor discovers a vulnerability in their own product
(no external finder) see #1168-vendor-as-finder (tracked separately).

## Rough Approach

- Finder reports vulnerability to Vendor1's inbox (RS message)
- Vendor1 validates, creates a case (CaseActor co-located or dedicated)
- Vendor1 invites Finder to the case
- Vendor1 determines Vendor2 is affected and invites them
- Vendor2 accepts and engages
- Embargo is established across F + V1 + V2
- All parties coordinate to fix, deploy, publish, and close

## Acceptance Criteria

- [ ] Scenario script runs deterministically end-to-end
- [ ] All three actors (F, V1, V2) are participants with correct CVDRole values
- [ ] EM state reaches ACTIVE with all three as SIGNATORY
- [ ] CS state progresses through VFD on all replicas
- [ ] Final state: VFDPxa + RM.CLOSED for all participants
